The IPhone is a line of smartphones developed and marketed by Apple that run iOS, the company's own mobile operating system. The first-generation iPhone was announced by then–Apple CEO Steve Jobs on January 9, 2007, at Macworld 2007, and launched later that year. Since then, Apple has annually released new iPhone models and iOS versions; the most recent models being the iPhone 16 and 16 Plus, and the higher-end iPhone 16 Pro and 16 Pro Max.

The iPhone 16 Pro Max, Apple's latest flagship, boasts a sleek design with a wider color palette, a powerful A18 Bionic chip, and an improved dual-lens camera system with a 48MP main sensor and 2x optical zoom. Its 6.1-inch Super Retina XDR OLED display supports HDR10 and Dolby Vision, while the larger battery offers up to 22 hours of video playback. Additional features include Wi-Fi 7, a USB-C port, an Action button, Spatial audio, and Emergency SOS via satellite.
